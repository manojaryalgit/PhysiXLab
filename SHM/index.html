Simple Harmonic Motion

About Simple Harmonic Motion

  • Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM) is a type of periodic motion where the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement.
  • The period of oscillation is given by T = 2π√(m/k), where m is mass and k is spring constant.
  • In the absence of damping, the total energy (kinetic + potential) remains constant.
  • The amplitude of oscillation depends on the initial displacement.
  • Damping causes the amplitude to decrease over time.
