Block Collision Simulator

How it Works

  • Enter the mass and initial velocity for each block.
  • The simulation will display the blocks moving and colliding.
  • The collision is calculated assuming an elastic collision (no energy loss).
  • After the collision, the blocks will move with new velocities based on the conservation of momentum and kinetic energy.
  • Negative velocity indicates movement to the left.

Physics Concepts

  • Momentum: The product of an object's mass and velocity. In a closed system, momentum is conserved.
  • Kinetic Energy: The energy of motion. In an elastic collision, kinetic energy is conserved.
  • Elastic Collision: A collision where both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved.
